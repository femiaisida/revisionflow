What is Revision Flow?

Revision Flow is a web app (installable as an app on your phone) that helps GCSE and A-Level students organise their revision, track progress, and use AI to study smarter. It is completely free to use.

Key features of Revision Flow

  • AI study plan generator — enter your subjects and exam dates, and Revision Flow generates a personalised revision timetable
  • Topic confidence tracker — rate every spec topic from 1–5 and see a heatmap of your strongest and weakest areas
  • Past paper grade tracker — log your past paper scores and Revision Flow automatically calculates your grade using real AQA, Edexcel and OCR grade boundaries (2019–2025)
  • Emergency Mode — exam in a few days? Emergency Mode gives you a predicted grade, your weakest topics, a today-only revision plan, and 3 predicted exam questions
  • AI answer marker — write a practice answer and the AI marks it like an examiner, with detailed feedback and a model answer
  • AI flashcard generator — generate revision flashcards for any topic and any exam board in seconds
  • Predicted exam questions — board-specific, topic-specific questions in the style of your actual exam
  • Revision timer — Pomodoro-style timer with ambient sounds and XP rewards
  • XP, badges and leaderboards — earn XP for every revision activity, unlock badges, and compete with friends

Which exam boards does Revision Flow support?

Revision Flow supports all major UK exam boards: AQA, Edexcel (Pearson), OCR, WJEC, Eduqas and CCEA. Both GCSE (grades 9–1) and A-Level (grades A*–E) are fully supported, along with BTEC Tech Award (Level 2) and BTEC National (Level 3).

Is Revision Flow free?

Yes. Revision Flow is completely free to use. There are no ads and no credit card required. A premium plan is planned for the future but the core features will always remain free.

How is Revision Flow different from other revision apps?

Most revision apps are either generic (not built around your specific exam board spec) or passive (you just read notes). Revision Flow is different in three ways:

  1. It uses real grade boundary data from AQA, Edexcel, OCR, WJEC and CCEA to calculate your actual grade — not an estimate
  2. It loads the exact topic list from your exam board specification, so you can track confidence on every single topic that could come up
  3. It uses Mistral AI with your actual data — your weak topics, your past paper scores, your exam dates — to give personalised advice rather than generic tips
